Training zones — know these cold
Easy (E)
Full conversation · nose breathing
7:20–7:45/mi
130–150 bpm
The majority of all your running. Builds aerobic base, repairs tissue, flushes fatigue. Never push easy runs faster — the benefit is the volume, not the pace. If it feels embarrassingly slow, it's probably right. Trust it.
Threshold (T)
Short sentences · comfortably hard
6:40–6:50/mi
162–172 bpm
Most important zone for this race. T-pace = lactate threshold — the pace you could hold for ~60 min max. Training here raises that ceiling. Notice it's nearly identical to your 6:45 race goal. This is the workout that wins the Peachtree.
Interval (I)
A few words · controlled hard
6:15–6:25/mi
174–185 bpm
VO2max pace. Done in short 1K reps with full rest between. Not a sprint — more like a focused 3-minute effort each rep. These raise your aerobic ceiling so 6:45 feels like a cruise on race day, not a fight.
HR zones are a guide, not gospel. Max HR formulas are wrong for trained athletes. Your actual max is likely higher. Trust feel first — if easy runs feel truly conversational around 145–150 bpm you're dialed. If 155 still feels easy, trust the feeling.
Drills
⚡ Do these before EVERY run · EVERY week · all 5 weeks
Takes 8 minutes. Not optional — especially with low back soreness. Drills activate hip flexors and glutes before each run, which is the #1 fix for low back issues in returning runners. Skip them and your back will let you know.
Leg Swings
10 reps each direction, each leg
Forward/back then side-to-side. Do this one first every time. Unlocks hip flexors — critical for your low back.
A-March
2 × 20m
Exaggerated high-knee march, pump arms. Activates hip flexors and sets proper knee lift mechanics.
A-Skip
2 × 20m
Same as A-march with a skip rhythm. Adds pop and ground contact timing on top of knee drive.
B-Skip
2 × 20m
High knee then extend and claw foot down. Teaches the pawing motion — the most important running mechanic.
Butt Kicks
2 × 20m
Heels to glutes, fast turnover. Trains hamstring recovery speed and high cadence muscle memory.
Strides
6–8 × 100m (when listed)
Smooth accelerations to mile effort. Full walk recovery between each. Maintain form — not a sprint.
